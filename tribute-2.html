About

Chien-Shiung Wu was a Chinese-born American physicist so accomplished and highly respected by her colleagues that she was nicknamed the First Lady of Physics and the Queen of Nuclear Research. In fact, she was even considered to be the Chinese Marie Curie.

Born in Jiangsu Province of China on May 31, 1912, Wu was raised by two uncommonly progressive parents who strongly believed in the importance of women's education and founded the Mingde School for Girls where Wu attended elementary school. Wu was so successful in school that she was offered a place at National Central University in Nanjing. Later on in her educational career, she earned her Ph.D. at the University of California Berkeley.

Wu was most well known for working on the Manhattan Project, as well as for debunking a hypothetical fundamental law of physics known as the conservation of parity. Despite her significant involvement in disproving the conservation of parity, her fellow colleagues, Tsung-Dao Lee and Chen-Ning Yang, were awarded the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1957 for the discovery, and Wu's experiments and research went unacknowledged. The Life of Chien-Shiung Wu

A Timeline

  • 1912

    Born in Jiangsu, China

  • 1930

    Attended National Central University

  • 1936

    Embarked on the SS President Hoover for the US. Arrived in San Francisco

  • 1940

    Completed her Ph.D. at University of California Berkeley

  • 1944

    Joined the Manhattan Project's SAM Laboratories at Columbia University

  • 1957

    Disproved the Conservation of Parity. Tsung-Dao Lee and Chen-Ning Yang were awarded the Nobel Prize for Physics.

  • 1963

    Confirmed the Conserved Vector Current Hypothesis of Richard Feynman and Murray Gell-Mann

  • 1965

    Published Beta Decay

  • 1975

    First female President of the American Physical Society. Awarded the National Medal of Science.

  • 1978

    First to receive Wolf Prize in Physics

  • 1990

    First living scientist to have an asteroid named after her

  • 1997

    Died in New York City, US
